### Meal 1 — Greek Chicken Bowls
*Monday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

New flavor alert: lemony, herby, with creamy yogurt sauce. Nothing from Plan 1 comes close.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Just Bare Chicken Chunks
- 1 cup Kirkland Jasmine Rice (dry)
- ½ cup Kirkland Greek Yogurt (plain)
- 1 lemon (juice + zest)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp garlic powder, salt, pepper
- Kirkland Frozen Stir-Fry Veg (1 cup, for bowl filler)

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Marinate chicken in lemon juice, olive oil, oregano, garlic, salt — 20 min minimum.
2. Cook in skillet on medium-high, 5–6 min per side until cooked through. Let rest, slice or keep whole.
3. Cook rice per package directions. Cool and refrigerate.
4. Mix yogurt + lemon zest + pinch salt for sauce. Store in small container.
5. Steam stir-fry veg, cool, refrigerate.

**Monday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat chicken in covered pan with 2 tbsp water, 8 min medium.
2. Reheat rice in microwave 2 min.
3. Reheat veg 2 min.
4. Bowl: rice base, chicken, veg, drizzle of yogurt sauce.

### Meal 2 — Korean-Style Ground Beef & Rice
*Tuesday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

Ground beef from Plan 1, totally different treatment. Sweet-savory Korean glaze, weeknight classic.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Ground Beef
- 1.5 cups Kirkland Jasmine Rice (dry)
- 3 tbsp Korean BBQ sauce or soy sauce + 1 tbsp brown sugar + ½ tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp garlic (minced), ½ tsp ginger (powder fine)
- 1 cup Kirkland Stir-Fry Veg (optional)
- Sesame seeds and sliced green onion for garnish (optional)

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Brown ground beef in skillet, breaking up. Drain fat.
2. Add garlic, cook 1 min. Add Korean BBQ sauce (or soy/sugar/sesame combo). Stir, simmer 2 min.
3. Cool and refrigerate in container.
4. Cook rice. Cool and refrigerate.

**Tuesday Dinner (15 min)**
1. Reheat beef in pan with splash of water, 5 min.
2. Reheat rice in microwave.
3. Serve beef over rice. Garnish if desired.

### Meal 3 — Salmon with Lemon-Herb Linguine
*Wednesday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

Salmon from Plan 1, totally new format — over pasta instead of rice. Elegant but fast.

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb Kirkland Salmon Fillets (2 pieces)
- 12 oz Barilla Linguine
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic (minced)
- Juice of 1 lemon + zest
- 1 tsp dried Italian herbs (basil + oregano)
- Salt, pepper, red pepper flakes (optional)
- Kirkland Frozen Broccoli (1 cup steamed, optional side)

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Season salmon with olive oil, salt, pepper, lemon zest.
2. Bake at 400°F, 12–14 min. Flake into large pieces when cool. Refrigerate.
3. Cook linguine to just under al dente (it'll finish when reheated). Toss with olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, herbs. Cool and refrigerate.
4. Steam broccoli, cool, refrigerate.

**Wednesday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Warm linguine in skillet with 2 tbsp water and splash of olive oil, 5 min medium.
2. Gently fold in salmon flakes. Heat through, 3 min.
3. Microwave broccoli 2 min.
4. Serve pasta topped with salmon + broccoli on side.

### Meal 4 — Thai Basil Turkey over Jasmine Rice
*Thursday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Ground turkey, different flavor universe than Plan 1's chili. Southeast Asian, punchy.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Kirkland Ground Turkey
- 1.5 cups Kirkland Jasmine Rice (dry)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp fish sauce (or extra soy + ½ tsp Worcestershire)
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp garlic (minced), ½ tsp red pepper flakes
- Kirkland Stir-Fry Veg (1 cup) — bell peppers and snap peas work best
- Dried basil (1 tsp) or fresh if available

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Cook turkey in skillet, breaking up. Drain if needed.
2. Add garlic, cook 30 sec. Add soy sauce, fish sauce, sugar, red pepper. Toss veg in. Cook 3 min.
3. Cool and refrigerate.
4. Cook rice. Cool and refrigerate.

**Thursday Dinner (15 min)**
1. Reheat turkey-veg mix in pan, 5 min.
2. Reheat rice in microwave.
3. Serve over rice. Add extra soy if desired.

### Meal 5 — Shrimp Fried Rice
*Friday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Shrimp from Plan 1, completely different format — fried rice, a family staple, fast on Friday.

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb Kirkland Raw Shrimp (thawed, tails off)
- 2 cups cooked Jasmine Rice (use leftover or cook fresh — day-old rice is best)
- 3 Kirkland Eggs
- 1 cup Kirkland Stir-Fry Veg (thawed, patted dry)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil or olive oil
- Garlic (2 cloves, minced)

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Cook rice, spread on sheet pan, refrigerate to dry out (better for fried rice).
2. Sauté shrimp in oil 2–3 min per side. Remove.
3. In same pan, cook garlic 30 sec. Add veg, cook 2 min. Push to side, scramble eggs.
4. Add cold rice, soy sauce, sesame oil. Toss everything. Add shrimp back. Cook 2 min.
5. Cool and refrigerate.

**Friday Dinner (15 min)**
1. Reheat fried rice in skillet with 2 tbsp water, 8 min on medium-high. Stir frequently.
2. Taste and add soy sauce if needed.

### Meal 6 — BBQ Pork Tenderloin Sandwiches
*Saturday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Pork tenderloin, different from Plan 1's roasted version. Pulled and saucy — crowd pleaser.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Kirkland Pork Tenderloin
- ½ cup Korean BBQ sauce or standard BBQ sauce (Costco carries Sweet Baby Ray's 2-pack)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp garlic
- Hamburger buns or dinner rolls (Costco bakery 12-pack)
- Kirkland Greek Yogurt (plain) — optional coleslaw base: yogurt + lemon + shredded veg

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Sear pork tenderloin in oven-safe pan on stovetop, all sides, 5 min total.
2. Brush with BBQ sauce mixture. Roast at 400°F for 18–22 min (internal temp 145°F). Rest 10 min.
3. Slice thinly or pull apart with forks. Toss with remaining sauce. Refrigerate.
4. Mix yogurt-lemon slaw if using (shred any stir-fry veg or cabbage with yogurt + lemon).

**Saturday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat pork in covered pan with 3 tbsp BBQ sauce and splash of water, 10 min medium.
2. Toast buns in oven 5 min at 350°F.
3. Serve pulled pork on buns with slaw.

### Meal 7 — White Bean & Chicken Soup
*Sunday · Serves 6–8 · Cook Day: Sunday*

A new format entirely — brothy, comforting soup. Plan 1 had chili; this is lighter and more versatile.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Just Bare Chicken Chunks
- 2 cans Cannellini or Great Northern Beans (drained, rinsed)
- 4 cups Kirkland Chicken Broth
- 1 can Diced Tomatoes (14.5 oz)
- 1 tsp garlic (minced)
- 1 tsp dried Italian herbs
- Kirkland Frozen Broccoli or Normandy Blend (1 cup)
- Salt, pepper, olive oil
- Parmesan for serving (optional — Costco sells Kirkland Parmesan wedges)

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Sauté garlic in olive oil 30 sec. Add tomatoes, cook 2 min.
2. Add broth, beans, herbs. Bring to simmer.
3. Add chicken chunks, cook 10–12 min until cooked through.
4. Add frozen veg last 3 min. Season. Cool and refrigerate.

**Sunday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat soup in pot over medium, 15 min. Add splash of broth if too thick.
2. Serve with crusty bread from Costco bakery.

### Meal 8 — Ground Beef Taco Bowls
*Monday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

Ground beef again — but bowl format, not burritos. Different toppings, looser format, faster assembly.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Ground Beef
- 1.5 cups Kirkland Jasmine Rice (dry)
- 1 packet taco seasoning (or: 1 tsp cumin + chili powder + garlic + onion powder)
- 1 can Bush's Black Beans (drained, rinsed)
- 1 can Diced Tomatoes (drained)
- Kirkland Greek Yogurt (plain) — as sour cream
- Shredded Mexican cheese (Costco 4-cheese blend)
- Lime juice (squeeze from pantry lime or bottled)

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Brown ground beef, drain fat. Add taco seasoning + 2 tbsp water, stir 2 min.
2. Separately, heat beans in small pan with cumin and salt, 5 min. Refrigerate.
3. Cook rice. Refrigerate.
4. Dice tomatoes if using fresh, or open canned. Refrigerate.

**Monday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat beef in pan, 5 min. Reheat rice and beans in microwave.
2. Bowl assembly: rice → beef → beans → tomatoes → yogurt → cheese → lime.

### Meal 9 — Lemon Herb Chicken Quinoa
*Tuesday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

Quinoa from Plan 1, completely new treatment. Light, Mediterranean, protein-forward.

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb Just Bare Chicken Chunks
- 1.5 cups Kirkland Quinoa (dry)
- Juice and zest of 1 lemon
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp garlic powder
- Kirkland Frozen Broccoli (1 cup steamed)
- Salt, pepper

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Season chicken with lemon juice, olive oil, oregano, garlic, salt. Cook in skillet 5–6 min per side. Cool and slice. Refrigerate.
2. Cook quinoa per package. Fluff, toss with lemon zest and olive oil. Refrigerate.
3. Steam broccoli. Refrigerate.

**Tuesday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat chicken in pan 8 min. Reheat quinoa and broccoli in microwave.
2. Serve chicken over quinoa with broccoli alongside. Extra lemon squeeze to taste.

### Meal 10 — Honey Garlic Salmon
*Wednesday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Saturday*

Salmon again — but honey garlic instead of teriyaki. Completely different flavor, equally fast.

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb Kirkland Salmon Fillets
- 2 tbsp honey (Kirkland Clover Honey is Costco staple)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic (minced)
- Kirkland Jasmine Rice (1 cup dry)
- Kirkland Frozen Stir-Fry Veg (1 cup)

**Cook Day Prep (Saturday)**
1. Mix honey, soy sauce, garlic in small bowl. Reserve 2 tbsp for serving.
2. Brush salmon with mixture. Bake 400°F for 12–14 min. Cool and refrigerate.
3. Cook rice. Cool and refrigerate.
4. Steam veg. Cool and refrigerate.

**Wednesday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat salmon in covered pan or microwave with a drizzle of water, 5 min gentle heat.
2. Reheat rice and veg in microwave.
3. Serve salmon over rice + veg. Drizzle reserved honey-garlic sauce on top.

### Meal 11 — Turkey Meatball Soup
*Thursday · Serves 6–8 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Ground turkey, completely different from Plan 1's chili. Meatball format — hearty, family-friendly.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Kirkland Ground Turkey
- 1 Kirkland Egg (binder)
- ¼ cup breadcrumbs or crushed crackers
- 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp Italian herbs, salt, pepper
- 4 cups Kirkland Chicken Broth
- 1 can Diced Tomatoes
- 1 cup Annie Chun's Udon Noodles (or broken linguine)
- Kirkland Frozen Broccoli (1 cup)
- Parmesan for serving

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Mix turkey with egg, breadcrumbs, garlic, herbs, salt, pepper. Form 1.5-inch balls.
2. Brown meatballs in skillet with olive oil, 4–5 min per side. They don't need to be cooked through.
3. In large pot: broth + tomatoes, bring to simmer. Add meatballs, cook 12 min.
4. Add noodles last 5 min. Add broccoli last 3 min. Season. Cool and refrigerate.

**Thursday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Reheat soup in pot 15 min medium. Add splash of broth if thickened.
2. Serve with Parmesan and crusty bread.

### Meal 12 — Shrimp Scampi with Linguine
*Friday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Shrimp again — but scampi format, garlicky butter sauce over pasta. Very different from fried rice.

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb Kirkland Raw Shrimp (thawed, peeled, tails off)
- 12 oz Barilla Linguine
- 3 tbsp olive oil (or 2 tbsp olive oil + 1 tbsp butter)
- 4 cloves garlic (minced)
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt, pepper
- Fresh or dried parsley

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Cook linguine al dente. Toss with olive oil. Refrigerate.
2. Sauté garlic in oil 1 min. Add shrimp, cook 2 min per side (just pink). Add lemon juice, red pepper. Toss. Cool and refrigerate separately from pasta.

**Friday Dinner (20 min)**
1. Warm pasta in skillet with 3 tbsp water and olive oil, 5 min.
2. Add shrimp to pan last 3 min — heat gently to avoid overcooking.
3. Toss together. Serve with extra lemon.

### Meal 13 — Pork Tenderloin with Apple Glaze
*Saturday · Serves 4–6 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Pork tenderloin again — but apple glaze instead of BBQ. Feels completely different. Elegant Saturday.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Kirkland Pork Tenderloin
- ¼ cup apple juice or apple cider
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp garlic (minced)
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional)
- Kirkland Frozen Stir-Fry Veg (2 cups) or Normandy Blend
- Kirkland Jasmine Rice (1 cup dry) or Quinoa

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Mix apple juice, soy sauce, honey, garlic, mustard. Reserve 3 tbsp.
2. Sear tenderloin in oven-safe pan. Brush with glaze. Roast 400°F for 18–22 min (145°F internal).
3. Rest 5–10 min. Slice, refrigerate. Save drippings.
4. Roast veg alongside with olive oil. Cook grain. Refrigerate.

**Saturday Dinner (25 min)**
1. Reheat pork in covered pan with drippings + reserved glaze + 2 tbsp water, 10 min.
2. Reheat veg and grain in microwave.
3. Serve sliced pork over grain + veg. Spoon glaze over top.

---

### Meal 14 — Chicken Noodle Casserole
*Sunday · Serves 6–8 · Cook Day: Sunday*

Comfort food finale for Week 4. Chicken + udon noodles + creamy sauce. Different from Plan 1's chicken parm.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lb Just Bare Chicken Chunks
- 2 packs Annie Chun's Udon Noodles (or 8 oz wide egg noodles)
- 1 cup Kirkland Chicken Broth
- ½ cup Kirkland Heavy Cream or Greek Yogurt (for lighter version)
- 1 can Diced Tomatoes (half can, drained)
- Kirkland Frozen Broccoli (1 cup)
- 1 tsp garlic powder, Italian herbs, salt, pepper
- Shredded Parmesan or mozzarella for topping

**Cook Day Prep (Sunday)**
1. Cook chicken in skillet with olive oil, garlic, herbs. Slice. Set aside.
2. Cook noodles, drain, set aside.
3. In same skillet: broth + cream, simmer 3 min. Add tomatoes (half can), mix.
4. Combine chicken, noodles, veg, sauce in oven-safe baking dish. Top with cheese.
5. Bake 375°F for 20 min until bubbling. Cool and refrigerate.

**Sunday Dinner (25 min)**
1. Reheat covered in 350°F oven for 20 min (or microwave 5 min).
2. Serve directly from dish.
