## Brand Identity

### Name and Tagline

Use the short-form tagline in the PDF and on the landing page.

---

### Color Palette

Use this 4-color palette. All are warm, approachable, and print cleanly.

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Costco Red | `#E31837` | Cover accents, CTA buttons, key highlights |
| Secondary | Warm Navy | `#1A3557` | Headers, section titles, body text |
| Accent | Golden Yellow | `#F5A623` | Icons, callout boxes, "savings" numbers |
| Background | Off-White Cream | `#FFF8F0` | Page background — warm not stark |

**Why this palette:** Costco's brand red creates immediate visual association without being a direct copy. The warm navy and cream give a premium-cookbook feel that justifies a paid product.

---

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weight | Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Page title | Playfair Display | Bold | 36–48pt |
| Section header | Playfair Display | Regular | 22–28pt |
| Body text | Lato | Regular | 11–12pt |
| Labels / callout | Lato | Bold | 10–13pt |
| Tagline | Playfair Display | Italic | 18–22pt |

Canva has both Playfair Display and Lato in its free font library. Do not substitute.

**Why this combo:** Playfair Display is a classic editorial serif — it reads as cookbook quality. Lato is clean and highly readable at small sizes. Together they signal "professional food content" not "generic AI document."

---

### Logo Treatment

**Option A (recommended — 30 minutes):**
Text logo only. Set "Stock Up Dinners" in Playfair Display Bold, Warm Navy. Add a small Costco-red shopping cart icon from Canva's free icon library to the left. That's it. Simple, scalable, works at all sizes.

**Option B (if you want more):**
Add a secondary line below in Lato Regular italic, Golden Yellow: *Costco-anchored meal prep*

No custom graphic design needed at this stage. The wordmark is sufficient.

## Template 1: PDF Cover Page

**Canvas size:** 8.5" × 11" (US Letter, print-ready)
**Background:** Warm Navy `#1A3557`

**Layout (top to bottom):**

1. **Logo** — top left, Stock Up Dinners wordmark in white/cream, 20pt
2. **Hero visual zone** — 40% of page height. Option A: a Canva stock photo of a Costco warehouse interior or meal prep containers. Option B: a simple flat-lay of ingredients (avocado, chicken, jasmine rice) on a cream background. Either works.
3. **Title** — "14 Costco Dinners for Busy Families" in Playfair Display Bold, white, 40pt, centered
4. **Tagline** — "One big trip. Two weeks of dinners." in Playfair Display Italic, Golden Yellow `#F5A623`, 20pt, centered
5. **Subtitle bar** — thin Costco Red `#E31837` horizontal rule, then: "The FREE Starter Plan from Stock Up Dinners" in Lato Regular, white, 12pt
6. **Footer** — stockupdinners.com in Lato Regular, white at 50% opacity, 10pt

- [ ] Test print Template 2 on paper to check readability at 11pt body text
- [ ] Export all 4 templates as PNG (for review) and PDF (for production)

## Notes on Stock Photos

For the cover page hero visual, use Canva's free stock photos. Search for:
- "meal prep containers" — shows organized prepared food
- "Costco warehouse" — creates immediate brand recognition
- "family dinner table" — lifestyle / warm / aspirational

If none of the free options work, Unsplash has excellent meal prep photography. Download one, upload to Canva.

Do not use: food photography that looks restaurant-quality or overtly staged. The aesthetic should be real, practical, achievable — not aspirational in a Food & Wine sense.
